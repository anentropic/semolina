    3. **Runtime behavior note** — One short paragraph: At query time, `Fact` and `Dimension` produce identical SQL (`SELECT "col" FROM ... GROUP BY ALL`). The distinction is conceptual — `Fact` signals intent, not different execution.

    4. **When to use** — Short paragraph or two short sentences:
       - Default to `Dimension`. `Fact` is an intentional opt-in for two situations:
         1. **Semantic precision** — the column is a raw event-level numeric (`unit_price`, `quantity`, `line_amount`) that you want to distinguish from categorical grouping attributes. This signals intent to teammates and future readers.
         2. **Snowflake alignment** — your warehouse semantic view uses a `FACTS` clause for this column. Use `Fact` to mirror that designation.

    5. **Code snippet** — Keep the existing snippet (unit_price, quantity) or update to also show a nearby `Dimension` for contrast. Keep it concise. The `# raw price column, not aggregated` comment style is appropriate.

    6. **Tabbed SQL** — Keep the existing Snowflake/Databricks SQL tabs unchanged (the SQL output is correct and identical to Dimension output, which is the point).

    **Anti-patterns to AVOID:**
    - Do NOT claim `Fact` generates different SQL than `Dimension` — they are identical.
    - Do NOT imply Databricks users should not use `Fact` — frame it positively (still useful for semantic precision).
